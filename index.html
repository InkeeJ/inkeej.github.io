Selected Projects

  • Predicting Aviation Accident Severity - The Erdős Institute - Summer 2025
    GitHub | Video | Slides
    Led data extraction and cleaning by converting .mdb files to .csv and integrating event, aircraft, and sub-aircraft tables into a unified dataset. Engineered and imputed features for classification and regression tasks. Built ensemble models (XGBoost, Random Forest, Extra Trees, Histogram Gradient Boosting) with grid search and cross-validation. Achieved an F1 score of 0.459 (Extra Trees) in aircraft damage classification, significantly above the 0.316 baseline.
  • Predicting untrustworthy predictions and the Law of Learning Dynamics - Spring 2025 - Present
    On-going project developing a geometric framework for untrustworthy samples including adversarial attacks using Logifold structures.
    Using Logifold structures to detect perturbations, enhance robustness, and improve ensemble performance.
  • Persistent Laplacian Diagrams - Spring 2025 - Present
    Developing a featurization method for the Persistent Laplacian analogous to the persistence image for persistent homology, and defining signatures of the Persistent Laplacian together with a stability analysis for the resulting Persistent Laplacian diagrams.
    Constructed an explicit pair of non-isomorphic graphs that are indistinguishable by persistent homology or classical graph Laplacian spectra, but distinguishable by our Persistent Laplacian based signatures, demonstrating their additional discriminative power.
    Currently extending this framework to biomolecular, point cloud, and graph datasets through a series of benchmark experiments.
